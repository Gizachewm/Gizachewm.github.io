Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. I am currently employed in Ethiopia and hold a work permit. Can I register for studies at UoG?
A1. Work permit holders can register for studies at UoG. Anyone on a work permit who has received an offer to study at UoG must approach the Department of Immigration and Foreign Affairs to obtainan endorsement to study at UoG, prior to commencing studies.
Q2. Before submitting an application to UoG, do I need to contact a supervisor/faculty member and request an informal approval that they would be supervising my Masters/PhD degree?
Q2. Yes – if you intend to study for a research degree at UoG is advisable to do this before hand toc onfirm that there is an academic to supervise your intended research project.

Q4. Is the degrees offered at UoG an internationally recognized degrees?
A4. Yes – all the degrees offered at UoG are internationally recognized.
Q5. Am I expected to send official transcript to UoG?
A5. Yes- applicants who applied for Masters and PhD programs and offered admission are expected to send their official from the University/institute they have completed their study directly to the University of Gondar Main Registrar via Postal Service or via the following official email address of the Main Registrar Office: Registrar@uog.edu.et. Only official transcripts directly sent by the institutes to UoG are acceptable.
Q6. What else should I am expected to know?
A6. Make sure that you have Letter of Certification (equivalence) from the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia Higher Education Relevance and Quality Agency (HERQA),for the certification of your educational documents.
